Synopsis

Growing up in the early 1800s, P.T. Barnum displays a natural talent for publicity and promotion, selling lottery tickets by age 12. After trying his hands at various jobs, P.T. turns to show business to indulge his limitless imagination, rising from nothing to create the Barnum & Bailey circus. Cast

Hugh Jackman, Zac Efron, Zendaya, Rebecca Ferguson, Keala Settle

Director: Michael Gracey


Review

By Brianna Mellissa T.: The Greatest Showman movie is exceptional, the songs have a lot of contrast with the situations that arise and help you to better understand the way people feel. Likewise, the actors do a great job, as they express themselves, making the viewer identify and empathize with them. Also the art that is reflected in the costumes and scenery is very nice, it makes you want to be there. The story is very good, it shows the life of someone who always sought to be better and more important, and also the consequences that this entailed, showing that it is not easy and that family should not be left aside and that it can also be found in other environments such as work. I recommend it because it is a film that you enjoy a lot, both visually and aurally. It is for all ages and the story is very good.
